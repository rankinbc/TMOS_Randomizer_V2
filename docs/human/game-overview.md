# The Magic of Scheherazade: Complete Knowledge Base

**An Arabian Nights action-RPG hybrid** developed by Culture Brain for the NES, this game uniquely blends Zelda-style top-down action with Dragon Quest turn-based battles. Released in Japan as "Arabian Dream Scheherazade" (1987) and North America (January 1990), the game features **5 chapters**, **11 recruitable allies**, **time travel mechanics**, and a password save system spanning approximately 750 screens.

---

## Game overview and core structure

The player controls an unnamed hero—descendant of the legendary magician **Isfa**—who must recover his lost memories after the evil wizard **Sabaron** curses him and scatters him through time. The main objective: rescue Princess Scheherazade and her three sisters from five demons, ultimately destroying the archdemon **Goragora** in the Dark World.

The game operates in two distinct combat modes:
- **Action Mode (ADVC)**: Real-time top-down combat using swords and magic rods
- **Turn-Based Mode (RPGC)**: Random encounters with party-based menu combat

**Three character classes** can be switched at Mosques: Fighter (strongest swords), Magician (strongest rods), and Saint (can use special protective equipment). Strategic class-switching is essential for progression.

**Level caps** exist per chapter—maximum 5 levels gained per chapter. After defeating each boss, the hero automatically advances to the next level tier.

---

## Chapter structure and progression

Each chapter functions as a self-contained world with unique themes, a captive to rescue, and a demon boss. **Players cannot return to previous chapters once completed.**

| Chapter | World Name | Setting | Captive | Boss Demon | Time Travel |
|---------|-----------|---------|---------|------------|-------------|
| 1 | **Mooroon** | Water/underwater | Princess Ashelato | Gilga | 50 years past |
| 2 | **Alalart** | Desert | Princess Ishutal | Curly | 300 years past |
| 3 | **Samalkand** | Forest/winter | Princess Roxanne | Troll | 30 years future |
| 4 | **Celestern** | Flower/volcanic | King Feisal | Salamander | 3000 years past |
| 5 | **Sabaron's Realm** | Dark World | Scheherazade | Goragora | 3000 years past |

**Chapter 1 (Mooroon)** introduces core mechanics with water-themed areas and mermaid towns. Key locations include Meshudo, Rudoria, Poponill, and the Palace of Aqua. The Great Magic **Monecom** sets currency to 999.

**Chapter 2 (Alalart)** features constant HP drain while traversing desert unless the Great Magic **Raincom** is used. The boss Curly requires ally Epin's whistle to reveal her true form. Gun Meca translates the ancient "Peke Peke" language.

**Chapter 3 (Samalkand)** uniquely travels to the future rather than past. The Cimaron Tree puzzle requires password "CHOCOLA" to obtain fruit for creating ally Pukin. Great Magic **Springcom** stops cold damage.

**Chapter 4 (Celestern)** has the most difficult boss—Fire Demon Salamander must be fought 3000 years in the past when newly born. Ally Rainy's rain drum draws him from his fire field. The **Rostam** sword (fires two projectiles) is found here.

**Chapter 5** culminates in confronting Sabaron, who reveals Scheherazade has been disguised as companion Coronya all along. The player must answer "CORONYA" when asked to identify her or face instant game over. Final boss Goragora requires **Bolttor3** and the **Isfa Rod** to defeat.

## Allies and party members

The game features **11 recruitable allies** who participate in turn-based battles, provide special abilities, and combine for formation attacks.

| Ally | Chapter | Special Ability | Formation Partner |
|------|---------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Coronya** | 1 (start) | Detects hidden staircases; secretly Scheherazade | Faruk (Cygnus) |
| **Faruk** | 1 (past) | Enables underwater breathing/swimming | Coronya, Hassan |
| **Kebabu** | 1 (present) | Provides Mirror Shield and Ring; enables boss escape | Gun Meca (Libra) |
| **Gun Meca** | 2 (present) | Translates ancient languages | Kebabu (Libra) |
| **Supica** | 2 (past) | Guides through Maze Desert | Epin (Aries) |
| **Epin** | 2 (present) | Whistle summons Curly's second form | Supica (Aries) |
| **Pukin** | 3 (future) | Cimaron Rod lights dark mazes | Mustafa (Sirius) |
| **Mustafa** | 3 (present) | Reveals Troll's true form; costs 100 Rupias | Pukin (Sirius) |
| **Gubibi** | 4 (present) | Provides Holy Robe for lava survival | Rainy (Kaitos) |
| **Rainy** | 4 (past) | Rain drum draws Salamander from fire | Gubibi (Kaitos) |

**Troopers** are mercenary soldiers purchased in groups of 4 for 100 Rupias (max 99). They fight automatically in turn-based battles, absorbing damage and attacking enemies. Trooper strength increases each chapter.

## Enemy types and bosses

### Overworld action enemies

Common field enemies include **Wasps** (basic flyers), **Yanri** (disguised wasps requiring two hits), **Piranhas** (leap from water), **Fishmen** (spit projectiles), **Thieves/Bandits** (ambush from bushes), and **Fana** (demons disguised as thieves).

**Desert enemies**: Scorpions (burrow underground), Kural/Keral (sand-dwelling creatures), Chameleons (camouflage).

**Cave/dungeon enemies**: Red/Blue Kibra (three-eyed runners), Hebel (spawn from huts), Red/Blue Rabita (dragon-like door guardians), Rodian (statues neutralized with Horn item).

**Plant enemies**: Gol (carnivorous flowers), Kodama (bouncing tree trunks), Oodama (split into three Kodamas).

**Fire enemies**: Abiru/Ifrit (flame ring demons), Fencer (palace patrollers).

**Special**: Grim Reaper (appears if lingering too long on certain screens; high HP but drops money bags), Boulders/Logs (environmental hazards).

### Turn-based battle enemies by strength tier

**Weak tier (3-12 HP)**: Meldo, Derol, Blimro, Samrima, Kakkara, Berlah, Miniyad

**Medium tier (16-42 HP)**: Amaries (water, casts Gilzade), Corsa (double-faced), Cytron (fire, casts Flamol1), Curgot/Dalzark (armored), Medusa (petrifies), Pandarm (bird men, fly to become invulnerable), Megarl, Gazeil (casts Flamol3)

**Strong tier (54-122 HP)**: Wazarn, Pharyad (casts Bolttor3), Romsarb, Gangar, Gigadan (72 HP, lava-based), Zahhark (reflects magic), Gorla (Chapter 5 only), Gorgon/GorgonX (88-122 HP)

**Enemy formations** that counter specific party formations:
- Basido Squad (Amaries + Corsa) → countered by Cygnus
- Air Squad (Pandarm + Corsa) → countered by Libra
- Fire Squad (Cytron + Megarl) → countered by Aries
- Gilas Regiment (Pandarm + Megarl/Romsarb) → countered by Sirius
- Magma Regiment (Gazeil + Romsarb) → countered by Kaitos
- Zodor Division (Wazarn + Razaleo) → countered by Dragon

## Key game mechanics for ROM analysis

**Character class system**: Three classes (Fighter ID, Magician ID, Saint ID) with different weapon effectiveness and equipment permissions. Class changes cost 40/60/100 Rupias at Mosques.

**Level system**: Experience-based with chapter caps (5 levels per chapter). Spells unlock at specific experience thresholds.

**Time travel**: Each chapter has Time Doors to past/future. Chapter 3 uniquely goes forward; others go backward.

**Alalart Solar Eclipse**: Periodic event enabling Great Magic, better casino odds, and Rupia Seed planting.

**Password system**: 35-43 character passwords encode all progress, stats, items, allies. Special debug passwords exist: "xW" (chapter X with max stats), "SOUND" (sound test), "END" (ending).

**Turn-based battle structure**: Three phases—Fight/Escape/Peace selection, party formation (2 allies), then combat with FIGHT/ROD/MAGIC/TROOPER/ESCAPE commands.
